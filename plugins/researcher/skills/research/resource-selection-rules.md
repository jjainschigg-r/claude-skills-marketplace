# Resource Selection Rules

Source criteria vary by research mode. Apply the rules for the active mode when evaluating candidates during Phase 4 (discovery) and throughout retrieval.

---

## Exploratory mode

**Goal:** Broad understanding. Prioritize diversity of source type and perspective over authority.

**Include:**
- Encyclopedia and reference sources (Wikipedia, official documentation)
- Long-form explainers and overview articles from any credible outlet
- Academic or institutional overviews (don't require peer-review, but prefer it)
- Practitioner perspectives (blogs, talks, case studies)
- Historical or foundational sources regardless of date

**Recency:** Not a primary criterion. Older foundational sources are valid if they remain accurate.

**Exclude:**
- Vendor marketing pages where the content is primarily promotional
- Duplicate coverage (if three sources say the same thing, one is enough)

**Source count:** Aim for variety — at least 3–4 distinct source types in the final candidate list.

---

## Directed mode

**Goal:** Evidence to support a specific argument. Prioritize credibility, recency, and quantitative content.

**Include:**
- Major analyst firms: Gartner, Forrester, IDC, Ovum, 451 Research, EY, PwC, Deloitte, McKinsey, Accenture
- Official government/standards bodies: NIST, EU Commission, ISO, CISA, etc.
- Credible trade and technology media: The Register, Ars Technica, InfoWorld, TechCrunch, ZDNet, Wired (for technology topics)
- Academic papers with direct relevance
- Primary survey data and state-of-industry reports (even from vendors, if methodology is disclosed)
- Partner organizations where applicable (confirm with research profile)

**Recency:** Strongly prefer 2024 or later. Accept 2022–2023 if the source is authoritative and no newer equivalent exists. Reject anything older unless it is a foundational reference.

**Quantitative bias:** Actively seek sources likely to contain statistics, survey data, adoption figures, cost data, or benchmark results. Prefer these over purely qualitative sources when both are available.

**Exclude:**
- Direct competitors (confirm exclusion list with research profile)
- Third-party summaries of analyst reports — go to the primary source
- Sources that cannot be verified as coming from the claimed organization's own domain

**Source count:** 12–15 candidates; expect ~8–10 successfully retrieved.

---

## Perspective mode

**Goal:** Authentic practitioner and community opinion. Authority is irrelevant — lived experience and candid views are the signal.

**Include:**
- Reddit (especially subreddits relevant to the topic — r/devops, r/kubernetes, r/MachineLearning, r/sysadmin, etc.)
- Hacker News (news.ycombinator.com) — search for relevant discussions
- dev.to, Medium (practitioner-authored, not brand-published)
- Personal engineering blogs and substack newsletters
- Stack Overflow and related Stack Exchange sites for technical opinion
- Conference talk summaries and retrospectives
- GitHub issue discussions where practitioners discuss real problems

**Recency:** Prefer the last 12–24 months for active communities. Older threads are acceptable if the discussion remains relevant and active.

**Exclude:**
- Official vendor documentation and marketing (this is the opposite of what we want)
- Analyst reports
- News articles summarizing vendor announcements
- AI-generated content farms (watch for thin, generic content with no personal voice)

**Search operators:** Use site-specific operators to target community platforms:
- `site:reddit.com <topic>`
- `site:news.ycombinator.com <topic>`
- `site:dev.to <topic>`
- `"<topic>" blog OR "my experience" OR "lessons learned" OR "in production"`

**Source count:** 10–15 candidates; community content is often shorter, so volume compensates.
